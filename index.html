Simulating Plasma

Particle-in-Cell (PIC) is the state-of-art computer algorithm to simulate the complex plasma dynamics.

Pulsars

  • Ultra-strong magnetic field: $10^{8}\,\mathrm{T}$, about a trillion ($10^{12}$) times higher than Earth's magnetic field.

  • Extremely compact: near 1.5 solar mass packed into about $10\,\mathrm{km}$.

  • Fast spinning: rotation period between a few seconds to a few milliseconds.

  • Surrounded by a magnetosphere filled with electrons $e^-$ and positrons $e^+$.

Pulsar Radio Emission


Pulsars drive constant $e^\pm$ pair production at their polar caps through QED processes in strong magnetic fields.


This process may be the key to solving the long-standing puzzle of pulsar radio emission.

Pulsar Radio Emission

Fully self-consistent QED Particle-in-Cell simulations of the polar cap pair cascade shows EM waves are generated, similar to the observed radio waves.



(Cruz, Chen, et al. 2021)
